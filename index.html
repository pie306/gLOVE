About

Our Product

GOAL

Our goal is to bring the world together through a simpler means of communication. A smaller set of glove based gestures will make the system far intuitive while expanding our product's applicability to a large variety of systems and situations.



DESIGN

Our product design intends to use a flex sensor for the palm and an accelerometer to control both directional movement as well as basic gestures. These components will be able to send 6 distinct gesture commands to both Bluetooth computers as well as android devices.



Control Applications

The eventual application will attempt to allow a user to control simple user inputs of their selection. For example, the space bar typically has the ability to pause a video. If mapped properly, the user would be able to pause and play the video. The large variety of possible options that come with control mapping means that the user has a great degree of flexibility.

Update #4

Prototype Development

April 27, 2015

Over the course of the past week, the parts arrived and prototype development began. In terms of the hardware design, the accelerometer, Arduino, battery, and Bluetooth transmitter were put together. The setup was tested on the first version of the Android application and was able to successfully pause and skip songs. The pc program prototype was also developed and was able to detect Bluetooth devices as well as rearrange the control scheme of the computer's inputs. Effectively, this week was a proof of concept with all the systems working properly together. Polish is still needed however.

Update #3

Product Purchasing and Android Development

April 20, 2015

This week we are waiting on our parts to arrive. We have ordered an Arduino uno as well as a Bluetooth transceiver to connect with a computer. The specific parts used are listed in the parts list on the website. In terms of sensors, we ordered a triple axis accelerometer and a flex sensor. Once the parts arrive we plan on testing the range of each of the sensors as well as testing the ADC’s on the Arduino board. While waiting for these parts to arrive, we spent time familiarizing ourselves with android development as well as how to connect devices via Bluetooth. In terms of languages and IDE we are writing our android code in java using android studio.

Update #2

List of Parts and Early Conceptual Designs

April 13, 2015

Researched various sensors, Bluetooth, android development, etc. Developed basic conceptual design of project. Specifically, decided project will likely use an accelerometer and flex sensor. We initially looked at using a magnetometer in conjunction with a multi axis accelerometer in order to be able to map pitch and yaw. However, after looking through different imu’s we determined that a magnetometer would be unrealistic given our budget as well as defeat the purpose of a cheap gesture recognizing glove. From there we decided to use a triple axis accelerometer and to implement a different scheme in order to recognize inputs in the y axis. As initially planned we are going to use a flex sensor to measure the bend of the hand. We will need some sort of microcontroller to process the data given by the various sensors as well as relay that data via Bluetooth to either a phone or computer. We still need to look into what controller or device to use. Also began looking into development of android application.
